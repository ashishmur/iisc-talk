# Part 3: Microsoft's AI Deployment Journey

**Source:** Voice memo from Apple Notes "IISC lecture" (memo 2, ~3.5 min)
**Recorded:** ~March 1, 2026

---

So one, we're definitely seeing what's happening in the software domain directly. Software engineers' productivity in terms of how much they've been able to get work done has almost been at 20x. So fair — industries are very interesting to me.

I think language models in the form of chat have always existed. Despite us even making Copilot, we ourselves found it very difficult to think about how do we use this technology in terms of driving productivity. To be able to onboard into AI thinking — of course there's a lot of talent itself which is continuously coming in. Mustafa Suleiman drives a big part of Microsoft AI as the CEO of Microsoft AI.

And how does this actually revolutionize work? There's been a lot of learnings for us in that journey. First, we figured: **you need to put the models into the hands of employees and allow employees to figure out what is the right new set of workflows or processes which get created because of the introduction of AI.** Trying to govern something which was top-down in terms of "this is how you're able to use it and increase productivity" just did not seem to work. We realized that there was a lot of nuance for the information worker. You need to think about how you're able to successfully deploy it within your own organization.

One of the things we started doing was to have **unlimited compute in the hands of product managers** for most of the products out there. And we just pushed folks to say, "You've got to put in designated time in terms of being able to figure out what you can do out of this." Incentive structures in the organizational structure around your ability to use this — because there was a belief that the more you use it and the more you understand the patterns which are helping us automate our own work, we figure out the right means and modes and mechanisms to actually want to build it out for customers.

So if you think about it, a lot of the R&D in terms of consumer AI and enterprise AI — not at the model level but at the application level — is continuously happening. And people are trying to figure out new ways to do things. The launch of ChatGPT — it became one of the fastest growing consumer products in human history ever. It goes to show how everyone's already trying to find new ways to work.
